# Predicting and Preventing Food Safety Failures in Boston Establishments

## Project Overview 
This research investigates whether the drivers of health code violations in Boston food establishments are geographical or operational behaviors. 
The goal of this study is to provide a framework that allows the City of Boston to transition to a proactive role in preventing these systemic health violations. 

## Research Question 
To what extent do geographical areas (Census tracts) predict the frequency of total health violations in Boston food establishments compared to restaurant-level operational behaviors, and how can predictive modeling be used to prevent systemic health code failures? 

## Key Findings
While raw counts suggest geographical clustering, modeling reveals that operational behaviors are true drivers of risk. Neighborhood income was statistically insignificant (p=0.68). Instead, the model that uses Operational Risk Factors (Debris, Hygiene, Pests) are the primary drivers, explaining 54.61% of the variance, proving food safety risk is highly predictable when focusing on internal kitchen culture rather than geographical factors.
